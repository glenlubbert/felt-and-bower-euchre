How to play

Euchre in a nutshell — you (South) and North vs. West and East.

The goal

Win tricks. First team to 10 points wins the match. Each hand is worth 1–4 points.

1 · Calling trump (the part people find confusing)

Before any cards are played, one suit is chosen as trump — the boss suit that beats all others. The team that picks trump are the makers and must win at least 3 of the 5 tricks, or they get "euchred" (the other team scores 2).

It works in up to two rounds, going clockwise from the dealer's left:

Round 1 — the turned-up card. One card sits face-up in the middle. On your turn you either Order Up (make that card's suit trump — the dealer then takes the up-card and discards one) or Pass.

Round 2 — name a suit. If everyone passes, the up-card is turned down. Now each player may name any other suit as trump, or pass. (You can't pick the suit that was just turned down.)

If all four players pass both rounds, the hand is re-dealt. When the buttons appear above your hand, that's your turn to call trump — pick the suit your hand is strongest in, or pass.

2 · The Bowers (the two best cards)

The Jack of the trump suit is the Right Bower — the single highest card. The other Jack of the same color is the Left Bower — second highest, and it counts as a trump card (not as its printed suit). Trump order, high to low: Right Bower, Left Bower, A, K, Q, 10, 9.

3 · Going alone

When you call trump you can tick Go alone. Your partner sits out and you play solo. Win all 5 tricks alone and you score 4 points.

4 · Playing a trick

The leader plays any card; everyone else must follow the led suit if they can (remember the Left Bower counts as trump). Highest trump wins the trick; if no trump was played, the highest card of the led suit wins. The winner leads the next trick.

5 · Scoring

Makers win 3–4 tricks: 1 point. All 5 (a march): 2. Alone and all 5: 4. Makers fail to get 3 (euchred): defenders get 2.

Reading the table

A glowing name tag shows whose turn it is. Speech bubbles pop up to show each player's bid (Pass / Order it up / Call ♠). The gold D chip marks the dealer. Dots under each name show tricks won this hand. Turn on 💡 Hints anytime for a suggested play, bid, and discard.
