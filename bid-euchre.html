How to play Bid Euchre

Single deck · 6 cards each · 6 tricks · you (South) & North vs. West & East.

The goal

Win tricks to score points. First team to 32 points takes the match.

1 · Bidding

After the deal, going once around starting left of the dealer, each player either passes or bids a number of tricks (3 to 6) that their team promises to win. Each new bid must be higher than the last, and the highest bidder becomes the declarer and names trump.

Stick the dealer: if everyone before the dealer passes, the dealer is forced to bid — no free redeal.

2 · The Bowers

The Jack of the trump suit is the Right Bower — the highest card. The other Jack of the same color is the Left Bower — second highest, and it plays as a trump (not its printed suit). Trump order high→low: Right Bower, Left Bower, A, K, Q, 10, 9.

3 · Shooting the Moon

The biggest bid is the Moon — you promise to win all 6 tricks by yourself while your partner sits out. Moon with a slide: your partner first passes you their single best card and you discard one, then play alone. A no-help moon (no slide) is harder and worth more.

4 · Playing a trick

The declarer leads first. Everyone must follow the led suit if they can (the Left Bower counts as trump). Highest trump wins; with no trump played, the highest card of the led suit wins. The winner leads next.

5 · Scoring

You chose the style in setup. By default each team scores the tricks it wins; if the declaring team falls short of its bid it's euchred and loses the number it bid instead. A made moon is a big bonus; a failed moon is a big penalty.

Reading the table

The glowing name tag shows whose turn it is. Bubbles show each player's bid (Pass / Bid 4 / Moon!). The gold D chip marks the dealer; dots under each name are tricks won this hand. Turn on 💡 Hints for a suggested bid, trump, and card.
